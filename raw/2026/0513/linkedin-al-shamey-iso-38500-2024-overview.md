# Overview of ISO/IEC 38500:2024

Source: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/overview-isoiec-385002024-mohamed-al-shamey-cxqvf
Author: Mohamed Al-Shamey, Published Jun 2, 2024

## Summary

ISO/IEC 38500:2024 is a standard providing guiding principles for the governance of information technology (IT) within organizations. Applicable to all types of organizations: public and private companies, government entities, and not-for-profit organizations, regardless of size or extent of IT use.

## Key Features

1. **Guiding Principles**: Principles for effective, efficient, and acceptable use of IT.
2. **Governance Framework**: Aligns with ISO 37000 and integrates IT governance into broader governance of organizations.
3. **Applicability**: Designed for governing bodies and those supporting them.
4. **Holistic Approach**: Emphasizes integration of IT governance with overall corporate governance frameworks.

## Key Components of ISO/IEC 38500:2024

### Principles for IT Governance
Guiding principles for evaluating, directing, and monitoring IT use. Ensures IT contributes positively to strategic goals.

### Framework Alignment
Adopts principles from ISO 37000, emphasizing that governance of IT is integral to overall organizational governance. Creates a unified governance approach.

### Implementation Guidance
Detailed guidance on implementing IT governance principles, focusing on practical steps to integrate IT governance into existing organizational structures and processes.

## Key Changes in 2024 Version
- Alignment with ISO 37000:2021 (Governance of organizations)
- Expanded from 6 to 12 principles
- Enhanced emphasis on stakeholder engagement, data-driven decisions, risk governance, and long-term viability
- Stronger focus on ethical behavior and social responsibility
