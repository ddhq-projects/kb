# Guiding IT Governance: A Deep Dive into the 12 Principles of ISO 38500:2024 Clause 5

Source: https://isoltx.com/article.php?id=24

## Introduction

The governance of Information Technology (IT) is a critical aspect of modern organizational leadership, ensuring that IT resources are aligned with business goals and delivering value responsibly and ethically. ISO 38500:2024 provides a comprehensive framework for IT governance, with Clause 5 outlining 12 principles that governing bodies should adhere to for effective IT governance.

## The 12 Principles of ISO 38500:2024

### 1. Purpose
The organization's reason for existence should be clearly defined and communicated. This includes detailing the organization's intentions toward the environment, society, and stakeholders. For IT, this means aligning technology initiatives with the broader organizational purpose.

### 2. Value Generation
Focuses on defining the organization's objectives for creating value. IT plays a crucial role by enabling new or improved products and services, enhancing operational efficiency, and supporting innovation. Governing bodies should ensure IT investments are strategically aligned to maximize value creation.

### 3. Strategy
Governing bodies direct and engage with organizational strategy to fulfil the organizational purpose. This involves integrating IT strategy with business strategy, leveraging technological advancements for strategic advantage, and remaining adaptable to changes in the IT landscape.

### 4. Oversight
Involves monitoring the organization's performance, ensuring it meets the governing body's expectations and complies with ethical and legal standards. For IT, this includes establishing policies, monitoring compliance, and ensuring IT systems support the organization's goals.

### 5. Accountability
Requires the governing body to demonstrate accountability for the organization's use of IT and hold those delegated with IT responsibilities accountable. Ensures decisions are made by individuals with appropriate authority and expertise.

### 6. Stakeholder Engagement
Emphasizes understanding and addressing the needs and expectations of all stakeholders—customers, employees, suppliers, regulators. In IT, this means technology solutions are designed with stakeholder input, enhancing satisfaction and trust.

### 7. Leadership
Involves setting a clear vision for the organization's use of IT and leading ethically and effectively. Highlights the need for strong IT governance leadership aligned with organizational values.

### 8. Data and Decisions
Recognizes data as a valuable resource for decision-making. Underscores the importance of data governance—ensuring data is accurate, accessible, and used responsibly. Emphasizes protecting data from misuse and ensuring compliance with data protection regulations.

### 9. Risk Governance
Involves understanding and managing risks associated with IT use—cybersecurity, compliance, emerging technologies. Governing bodies should ensure a robust risk management framework identifies, assesses, and mitigates IT-related risks.

### 10. Social Responsibility
Requires organizations to consider broader societal impacts of their IT use. Addresses data privacy, digital inclusion, and environmental impact of IT infrastructure.

### 11. Viability and Performance Over Time
Emphasizes the need for organizations to remain viable and perform effectively over time. Involves ensuring IT systems are resilient, adaptable, and aligned with long-term strategic goals.

### 12. Ethical Behaviour
Requires organizations to act ethically in all IT-related activities. Ensures IT systems respect rights and privacy, comply with legal requirements, and do not harm stakeholders or society.

## Conclusion

By adhering to these 12 principles, organizations ensure IT systems and practices are aligned with strategic objectives, deliver value, and operate responsibly and ethically. These principles serve as a foundation for robust IT governance in the digital age.
