# ITIL 4 vs COBIT 2019 vs ISO 20000 — Detailed Comparison

## Overview Table

| Aspect | ITIL 4 | COBIT 2019 | ISO 20000 |
|--------|--------|------------|-----------|
| **Type** | Best practices framework | Governance framework | Certifiable standard |
| **Developed by** | AXELOS (PeopleCert) | ISACA | ISO/IEC |
| **Primary Focus** | Service value, customer experience, operational excellence | IT-business alignment, risk, compliance, governance | SMS requirements, auditable processes |
| **Scope** | IT services (34 practices) | Enterprise-wide IT governance (40 objectives) | IT and non-IT services (SMS lifecycle) |
| **Key Components** | Service Value Chain, 4 Dimensions, 7 Principles | 6 Principles, 5 Domains, Goals Cascade | 10 Clauses, 14+ core processes |
| **Mandatory?** | No — adopt what works | No — tailor to needs | Yes — all requirements mandatory |
| **Certification** | Individual (Foundation, MP, Strategist) | Individual (CGEIT) + organizational maturity | Organizational (SMS certificate, 3-year validity) |
| **Implementation Time** | 6-12 months | 12-18 months | 12-18 months |
| **Implementation Cost** | $50K-$200K | $100K-$500K | $30K-$100K |
| **Auditable?** | No | Through CMMI levels | Yes — Stage 1 & Stage 2 audits |
| **Best for** | IT ops teams needing service excellence | Boards needing governance and compliance | Organizations needing external validation |

## When to Use Each

### Choose ITIL 4 when:
- IT operations team (10+ people) needs structured service management
- SLA management, incident response, or change control needs improvement
- Digital transformation initiative with DevOps/Agile adoption
- MSP managing services for multiple clients
- Goal: reduce downtime, improve customer satisfaction, increase operational efficiency
- **Avoid:** DevOps-only teams, tiny startups (too heavy)

### Choose COBIT when:
- Public company with board oversight requirements
- SOX, GDPR, or regulatory compliance obligations
- Need to prioritize IT investments and demonstrate value
- Enterprise risk management is a priority
- Organization has 100+ employees with complex IT landscape
- **Avoid:** Small organizations without regulatory needs

### Choose ISO 20000 when:
- Customers or contracts require certified ITSM
- Government bids or regulated industry participation
- Already have ITIL in place and want external validation
- MSP/IT service provider wanting to prove maturity
- Industries: finance, healthcare, government, telecom
- **Avoid:** No external validation needed, internal-only IT

## Common Combinations

Organizations often combine frameworks:

1. **ITIL + ISO 20000** (most common): Use ITIL for guidance on how to implement ITSM practices, then certify against ISO 20000 to prove compliance. ITIL tells you "how," ISO tells you "prove it."

2. **COBIT + ITIL**: Use COBIT for governance-level oversight (what to do and why), ITIL for operational execution (how to do it). COBIT ensures IT aligns with business goals; ITIL ensures services run well.

3. **COBIT + ITIL + ISO 20000** (full stack): COBIT for governance, ITIL for operations, ISO 20000 for certification. Common in large enterprises and regulated industries.
