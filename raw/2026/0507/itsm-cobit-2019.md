# COBIT 2019 — Detailed Overview

COBIT 2019 is a framework developed by ISACA for the governance and management of enterprise information and technology (I&T). It helps organizations align IT with business goals through customizable processes, objectives, and maturity models.

COBIT 2019 builds on COBIT 5 by introducing greater flexibility, six governance principles, and 40 objectives separated into governance (setting direction) and management (execution).

## Six Design Principles

1. **Provide Stakeholder Value** — governance objectives cascade from stakeholder needs (enterprise goals → alignment goals → governance/management objectives). Uses a Goals Cascade mechanism.
2. **Holistic Approach** — considers all enterprise governance enablers, not just processes. Seven components work together.
3. **Dynamic Governance System** — governance is not static; it evolves with the organization, technology, and business environment.
4. **Distinct Governance from Management** — clear separation: governance evaluates/directs/monitors, management plans/builds/runs/assesses. This is a critical distinction.
5. **Tailored to Enterprise Needs** — adaptable via design factors (enterprise strategy, risk profile, compliance requirements, IT role, technology trends, etc.).
6. **End-to-End Governance** — covers full scope of enterprise IT, not just specific departments or functions.

## Governance vs. Management

### Governance Domain: EDM (Evaluate, Direct, Monitor)

Governance is the responsibility of the board. Three key functions:

- **Evaluate** — stakeholder needs, conditions, options, IT performance against goals
- **Direct** — through prioritization and decision-making (strategies, policies, resource allocation)
- **Monitor** — performance, compliance, and progress against agreed direction

### Management Domains

Management is the responsibility of the executive. Four domains handle planning, building, running, and assessing IT operations.

| ID | Objective | Description |
|----|-----------|-------------|
| EDM01 | Ensured Governance Framework Setting and Maintenance | Analyze requirements for IT governance and maintain components with clear authority, responsibility, and accountability. |
| EDM02 | Ensured Benefits Delivery | Optimize value from IT investments, processes, assets, and services by tracking benefits realization. |
| EDM03 | Ensured Risk Optimization | Ensure risk appetite is understood, IT risks are identified, managed, and optimized. |
| EDM04 | Ensured Resource Optimization | Ensure sufficient and adequate IT capabilities (people, process, technology) at optimal cost. |
| EDM05 | Ensured Stakeholder Engagement | Identify stakeholders, ensure transparent IT performance reporting, address concerns. |

## Seven Components (formerly Enablers)

1. **Processes** — structured sets of activities to achieve objectives
2. **Organizational structures** — roles and responsibilities for decision-making
3. **Information flows** — data required for governance and management
4. **People, skills, and competencies** — human resources needed
5. **Principles, policies, and frameworks** — direction and rules
6. **Culture, ethics, and behavior** — organizational culture supporting governance
7. **Services, infrastructure, and applications** — IT resources and capabilities

## Goals Cascade

COBIT uses a Goals Cascade to link stakeholder needs to specific governance and management objectives:

1. **Stakeholder drivers** → Enterprise goals (e.g., stakeholder value, business growth)
2. **Enterprise goals** → Alignment goals (e.g., IT value delivery, risk optimization)
3. **Alignment goals** → Governance and management objectives (EDM, APO, BAI, DSS, MEA)

This cascade ensures that every IT activity can be traced back to stakeholder value.

## Performance Management (CPM)

COBIT Performance Management (CPM) is based on CMMI capability levels:

| Level | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 0 | Incomplete | Process not implemented or fails to achieve objectives |
| 1 | Performed | Process implemented and achieves objectives |
| 2 | Managed | Process is planned, monitored, and adjusted |
| 3 | Defined | Process is standardized and follows documented standards |
| 4 | Measured | Process is quantitatively managed with measurable objectives |
| 5 | Optimizing | Process is continuously improved to meet current and future needs |

## Design Factors for Tailoring

COBIT 2019 introduces design factors to tailor the framework:

- Enterprise goals
- Enterprise risk profile
- IT-related issues
- Threat landscape
- Compliance requirements
- Enterprise size
- IT role model (supporting/leading)
- Sourcing model
- Technology adoption strategy
- Implementation method

## COBIT vs ITIL vs ISO 20000

| Aspect | COBIT | ITIL | ISO 20000 |
|--------|-------|------|-----------|
| Primary Focus | Governance, alignment, risk | Service management, operations | Certifiable SMS standard |
| Audience | Board, CIO, auditors | IT ops, service managers | All IT, certification bodies |
| Scope | Enterprise-wide IT governance | IT services | IT/non-IT services |
| Output | Maturity assessment | Best practices | Certification |
| Cost | $100K-$500K | $50K-$200K | $30K-$100K |
| Time | 12-18 months | 6-12 months | 12-18 months |
