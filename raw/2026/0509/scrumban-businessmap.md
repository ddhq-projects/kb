Scrumban operates on several foundational principles. Workflow visualization, WIP limits, flow-based management, explicit process policies.

Key practices: Scrumban board with deeper workflow stages, WIP limits per column, trigger-based planning (plan when WIP falls below threshold), flow-based metrics (cycle time, throughput) instead of velocity.

Works well for mixed products in fast-moving environments, long-term projects requiring gradual optimization, situations with frequent emergencies and urgent requests. Less effective for strict control, fixed deadlines, shared resources across multiple teams.
